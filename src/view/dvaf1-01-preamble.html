Before you begin

  • This online form can take an hour or more to complete. You can apply yourself. You can also ask a friend or lawyer to apply on your behalf. In some situations, the police may apply for you.
  • You may find answering these questions difficult because we will ask questions about your situation—what happened and when—what protection you need.
  • At the end of this guide you will have the necessary forms (possibly 2 or 3) to take to court.
  • Once you lodge the forms at court, the person you are wanting protection from will get a copy of your application.

Going to court

  • You need to lodge the forms and all attachments at a magistrates court. You can do this yourself, or you can ask a friend or family member to do so on your behalf.
  • You will be given a date to appear in court (usually in about 4 weeks time).
  • Your court date can be sooner if you also ask for an urgent temporary protection order. You have to explain why you need this.
  • The respondent will be given a copy of the application which includes a notice to appear in court at the same time.
  1. Do you need legal advice? You may wish to get legal advice before or after preparing your forms to get a protection order.
    • You can talk to a lawyer about your application before or after you lodge it with the court

How can I get legal advice?

You can get legal advice from:

  • Legal Aid Queensland – call 1300 65 11 88 for free legal advice
  • a community legal centre – go to www.qails.org.au or www.legalaid.qld.gov.au or call 1300 65 11 88 to check services in your area
  • a private solicitor – call the Queensland Law Society or visit www.qls.com.au for names of solicitors who can help.
  • In some areas there are programs to help people apply for a domestic violence order — these include domestic violence prevention programs, application assistance programs or domestic violence services. You can ask at the magistrates court about these programs.
