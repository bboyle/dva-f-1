What conditions are on a protection order?

All protection orders have two compulsory conditions which say that:

  1. The respondent must be of good behaviour towards the aggrieved and not commit domestic violence
  2. The respondent must be of good behaviour towards any named person and not commit associated domestic violence against the named person.

You can ask the court to include in extra conditions.

  1. Protecting {{aggrievedYour}} family and friends
    1. {{DoesTheAggrieved}} have children who need protection from {{respondentName}}?

    2. You must provide clear reasons why {{theChildren}} should be named on the order

      A child may be included on an order if the court is satisfied that naming the child on the order is necessary or desirable to protect the child from associated domestic violence, or being exposed to domestic violence committed by the respondent. Associated domestic violence is where domestic violence is committed against a child, relative or associate of an aggrieved.

    3. Has {{respondentName}} behaved in a way that is abusive, threatening or unwelcome towards people {{aggrievedYouKnow}}? Includes {{theAggrieveds}} family, relatives, friends, coworkers or other people {{aggrievedYouKnow}}. They are known as {{theAggrieveds}} associates.
    4. Has {{respondentName}} behaved in way that is abusive, threating or unwelcome towards you? You are also an associate of {{aggrievedName}}.
    5. Do they need protection from {{respondentName}}?

    6. You must explain why the {{theAssociatesNeed}} to be named on the order

      You need to provide clear reasons why it is necessary or desirable to protect {{aggrievedYour}} relative or associate from associated domestic violence.
